ASX

Australia’s S&P/ASX 200 briefly turns positive after a 1.5% intraday fall.

The recovery has been led by the major banks, particularly CBA.

Despite the broadly unchanged “dotplot” of the Fed, which caused the US dollar strength and “risk-off” moves overnight, investors seem keen to park their cash in the market before Christmas, particularly given the extreme selloff of late.

Expect the index to test the downtrend line and 100-day moving average near 5200 while it remains above today’s low at 5026.6.

Index last down 0.1% at 5097.

The local market has bounced back from a 1.4 per cent fall at the open, with Telstra, CSL and the major banks leading the rebound.

At 11:40am AEDT the S&P/ASX 200 was 0.6 per cent weaker for the day at 5073.2 points.

Rio Tinto fell 3.1 per cent and BHP Billiton is down 2.5 per cent despite a rise in the iron ore price overnight.

Telstra and CSL gained 0.3 per cent and the big four banks were mixed, with CBA and Wespac only slightly weaker while NAB and ANZ lose over 1 per cent.

Ord Minnett initiated Blackmores research coverage today with a Buy rating and $230 price target, 17% above the current level around $205.9 a share – a new record for the stock.

Are they too late the party? It may seem so, given that the vitamin maker is up 481% this year – the best performer in the S&P/ASX 200.

But the key is that Blackmores’ normalized earnings per share is expected to surge almost five-fold in the next three years.

That will see its PE ratio drop from a pricey 74.1 times in FY15 to a cheap 15.4 times in FY18.

A2 Milk jumped 35% to $1.40 a share after boosting its earnings guidance to a range of $33 million to $37 million, as much as 68% above prior guidance of $22 million.
